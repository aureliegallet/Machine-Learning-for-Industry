What is this about and why is it important?

NO2

NO2, also known as nitrogen dioxide, is a gas mainly produced during combustion, and it can contribute to the formation of other pollutants, like O3.

Exposure to high levels of NO2 can cause respiratory problems, especially for children, the elderly, and people with asthma. It can also weaken the bodyâ€™s defense against respiratory infections.

Additionally, NO2 contributes to acid rain and nutrient pollution in coastal waters, impacting both natural ecosystems and human-built environments. Monitoring its levels helps protect both health and the environment.

O3

O3, also known as ozone, is a gas that can be emitted by vehicles, factories, the usage of fossil fuels, combustion of materials, and many other sources.

While ozone high up in the atmosphere protects us from UV rays, at ground level, it is a harmful pollutant that can cause chest pain, coughing, and respiratory diseases. High ozone levels can particularly impact outdoor workers and active individuals.

Ozone also affects vegetation and ecosystems, reducing crop yields and forest growth. By keeping track of ozone levels, we can work towards reducing its sources and minimizing its harmful effects on both health and the environment.
